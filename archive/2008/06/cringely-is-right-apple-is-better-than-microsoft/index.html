Cringely is right, Apple is better than Microsoft

Posted on June 13, 2008 by jim Posted in Geek — No Comments ↓

Like everybody else on the planet or in near-Earth orbit I followed the Apple WWDC bandwagon avidly this week. No doubt the rest of the universe will get on the same bandwagon in various numbers of light-years’ time and be equally excitable.

I certainly wasn’t disappointed in what was offered. For a start, Apple will be making the iPhone I actually wanted (as one of these sophisticated 3G European buyers). I’ll be able to surf fast and find my way around thanks to GPS, while I’m listening to tunes through my choice of headphones (Shure SE530s – they’re great). As will my wife because she’s getting one too – which is important, as I’ll show.

But the iPhone wasn’t the main thing that turned my head and had me willing the shell out hard-earned on Apple kit. Oh no. The thing that got my interest is MobileMe. Sure, I can do everything that MobileMe does today by cobbling together a bunch of other Web-based services (modulo contacts, but I guess Google will solve that soon enough) like Google Mail/Calendar, Flickr, and a helping hand from Spanning Sync on the client side.

Yes indeed I can do that, but my soon to be iPhone owning wife can’t (or won’t). And this is the killer – marketing MobileMe as “Exchange for the rest of us” makes it simple and convenient for the technophobe-ish, and leaves me as the only tech-literate person in my household one less bit of cobbling together to perform. I’ll even pay the $150 or so US pesos for the family bundle because right now that’s pretty cheap for me to have no headaches and data everywhere I need it to be, without any of the hassle. There will be a seamless offering that my wife will use without thinking, and without having to resort to a bunch of third party applications and services which means no hassle for me. And I am willing to pay for that privilege.

Of course Microsoft has its Live Mesh (sounds like saying “Live Mess” when you’re drunk, I’m sure that’s not intentional) , but for some reason it just doesn’t appeal to me. It’s marketed as some cloud thing and ironically hasn’t got that “oh yeah, I get it appeal” of the Apple “Exchange for the rest of us” message. Which means that Cringely is right – the multipronged approach that Apple is taking is a winner. It’s going to budge me, a tight fisted geek who can get by without any paid-for service, into a happy consumer of high-quality paid-for services. And I’m going to feel good about it because the “experience” will be exceptional.
