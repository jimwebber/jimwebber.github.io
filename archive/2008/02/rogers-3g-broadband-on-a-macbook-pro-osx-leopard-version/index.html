Rogers 3G Broadband on a MacBook Pro (OSX Leopard Version)

Posted on February 23, 2008 by jim Posted in Uncategorized — No Comments ↓

I’ve been in Canada on and off since before Christmas working on some interesting large-scale network systems. Since I’m mobile a fair amount I’ve been trying to get my hands on a wireless broadband card for my MacBook pro. This has proven harder than I would have thought, but finally I’m there.

My employer got me a data contract with Rogers, and a PCMCIA card that I can’t use in my MacBook pro, so instead I had to order an ExpressCard modem from Expansys.

I have to say that Rogers technical support is utterly rubbish and only left me hanging on the phone for a long time before speaking to people who told me ridiculously dumb things like that Rogers doesn’t allow Macs on the network.

Right, now that’s off my chest I can get onto how to get broadband connectivity on the Rogers network.

  1. Buy a 3G (UTMS) modem. I use the Merlin X950D which has been fine for the couple of hours that I’ve owned it.
  2. Buy a data plan SIM from Rogers. As I said, my company bought me one with a PCMCIA card that I can’t use in my MacBook, but I suspect you can buy the SIMs as part of a data plan without the additional superfluous Rogers hardware.
  3. Download the drivers for MacOSX from the Merlin web site and install them from the .DMG
  4. Shove your card in and let it do its funky thing for a few minutes until the lights settle down.
  5. Go to Apple Menu, then System Preferences
  6. Open Network, and you should see your hardware listed on the left hand side.
  7. Select your hardware and click advanced.
  8. At this point you have to be a little creative. For me using the X950D, I chose Sony Ericsson for my hardware not Merlin because it’s not listed, and not Other because that didn’t work.
  9. I left the model as GRPS (GSM/3G) which is just fine.
  10. The APN field has to read: internet.com (remember this is Rogers in Canada, your APN may be different depending on your network provider)
  11. Then hit OK and go back to the network panel. You should now just be able to hit connect to get on the network.

The LED on my Merlin turns solid cyan at this point and surfing commences at about 1.5Mb/s, which is pretty nice.
