Rogers 3G Broadband on a MacBook Pro (Windows Vista Version)

Posted on February 23, 2008 by jim Posted in Uncategorized — No Comments ↓

So if you’ve got your modem working on OSX now you’ll want to get it running under Windows Vista on bootcamp, if you’re still bootcamping that is.

Understand that I am much more familiar with Windows than Mac OS and still it took me about 3 times as long to figure this out. I haven’t had to look up modem settings in Windows since the last century, and yet that’s what you have to do to get this up and running.

Vista SP1 has done nothing to stop my slow slide towards the Mac. But I digress. here’s the goods:

1. Slot in the Merlin X950D and let it install the drivers or just get them from the Web again and avoid installing that hopeless Mobilink software. Install the drivers.
2. Go to control panel and then phone and modems.
3. Click on the modems tab and then double click on the Merlin modem.
4. Click on the advanced tab.
5. In the extra initialization commands box, enter: +cgdcont=1,”IP”,”internet.com”
(the crazy initialisation string you should never have to remember, which also conflates the APN (internet.com) right in there. Bah.)
6. Hit ok as many times as you need to get back to the control panel.
7. Go to Network and Sharing centre.
8. Click on “Set up a connection or network”
9. Click “Set up a dialup connection” hit next and type in the phone number: *99#
10. Give the connection a meaningful name (like say “Rogers”). No need for any usernames, passwords, or any of that stuff for this connection. It’s all in the SIM card.
11. Connect to a network in the normal way. For example control panel, network and sharing, connect to a network and select the connection you just created.

Finally you get to connect to the network and enjoy Vista at its full blistering speed[*].

* Sarcasm, obviously. Vista feels like it is immersed in treacle even when you’re trying to do not particularly complex tasks.
