The Tongariro Crossing

Posted on January 18, 2007 by jim Posted in Uncategorized — No Comments ↓

After Wellington we headed to the picturesque lakeside town of Taupo, our base for a few days as we aimed to walk the Tongariro crossing one of the best day hikes in New Zealand weighing in at 17km with around 1km of vertical over some rough terrain (which makes it reasonably challenging too).

Yesterday morning we awoke unspeakably early and climbed into the bus to get over to the starting point for the hike at Mangatepopo. I confess I was half asleep as we walked from Mangatepopo to Soda Springs, up to the point where we could clearly see the imposing silhouette of Mount Ngauruhoe (aka Mount Doom from the Lord of the Rings) and the snow-capped playground of Mount Ruapheu. After that a good 30 minute vertical scramble up the “Devil’s Steps” seriously woke me up enough to require an emergency samosa stop at the top. Mmmmmm samosas.

From the top of the Devil’s Steps we moved on into the moonscape-like world of the south crater and up to the crater rim. Very reminiscent of a Dr Who set from the Tom Baker years!

After the south crater, there’s another (less challenging) climb up to the Red Crater which is an astonishing velvet red – not that the low light or my ageing camera shows it too well.

From the Red Crater there’s a scramble down some loam for a lunch stop the Emerald Pools (we were hungry!). The Emerald Pools are a set of beautiful green lakes of water and poisonous heavy metals, which are lovely to look at, awful for drinking and swimming. Not that it was warm enough for swimming yesterday anyway!

Once past the lakes, it’s a mostly cruisy downhill to the Ketetahi hut looking out over the lakes and mountains. After a quick chocolate stop at the hut, we rolled on down the hill. Actually I literally rolled on when, on a piece of paved track of all places, I rolled my ankle, went head first into a bag of conveniently placed gravel and bashed my knee. Needless to say the rest of the descent was much slower. I felt quite embarrassed being passed on the way down by a bunch girls in hotpants…and didn’t even manage to get a photo!

Today’s been a bit more chilled, we headed into the Taupo Hot springs to try to soak away the pain in my knee and ankle, and as I sit in the gardens of the lodge we’re staying at (and after installing a wireless router) it seems the springs have worked wonders.
