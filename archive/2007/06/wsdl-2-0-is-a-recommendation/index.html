WSDL 2.0 is a Recommendation

Posted on June 27, 2007 by jim Posted in Uncategorized — No Comments ↓

Â So WSDL 2.0 is finally (and boy do I mean finally) a recommendation.

The bullying and name-calling from the RESTafarian jihad over the years (yes it took years) has clearly had an impact with all manner of oddities like transferring a “safe” SOAP message to a service via GET. Makes sense in a Web architecture, while uncomfortably blurring the lines between transport (usually HTTP) and transfer (SOAP envelope and processing model) protocols.

Other notables include the admission that WSDL really is a verbose object IDL with the renaming of portType to interface, and the resolute determination to make it all map nicely onto Java classes despite overwhelming evidence that doing so is an unforgivably bad idea.

So on the back of the W3C’s recommendation, here’s another: stick with WSDL 1.1.

At least WSDL 1.1 has the single virtue your current toolset supports it, even if all you can do with it is RPC. Can’t even do that with 2.0 just yet, but at least it integrates with WTF RDF.

[Hey, I got through the entire post without mentioning that SSDL is far simpler and more expressive than any version of WSDL. Oh, wait a minute…]
