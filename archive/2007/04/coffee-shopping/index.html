Coffee Shopping

Posted on April 23, 2007 by jim Posted in Uncategorized — No Comments ↓

Having lived in Sydney for a good few years I really got into drinking good coffee. One reason was definitely because Aussies can’t do a decent cuppa tea (yeah, you blokes know it’s true), but the main reason is good coffee is available practically everywhere you turn in Sydney and Melbourne.

On returning to the UK it became pretty apparent that Starbuck’s, Costa, Caffe Nero and their ilk simply weren’t going to cut it. A good barrista is not a commodity, and being a barrista certainly isn’t some part time McJob. A good barrista doesn’t scorch milk or use soured beans; a good barrista knows their crema from their elbow and makes rich deep coffee, providing the social lubrication so critical to the city’s functioning.

Now it happens that London is a big city, and so it’s a statistical likelihood that there’s decent coffee available here too. The problem is simply one of locating  and enjoying it. As a relative newbie to the city, I’m finding out the hard way where the good places are, and so I’m recording my coffee explorations on a Google map.

The rules for getting on the map are pretty easy, I have to have a good take-away coffee at a reasonable price and it has to be repeatable. I’m open to visiting chains or independent stores or even bars so if you have a favourite place that makes the grade let me know and I’ll give it a go when I’m working in the area.

You’ll also have the warm satisfaction of knowing that my colleagues will have a much happier day around me :-)
