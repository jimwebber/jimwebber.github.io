Snowboarding in Whistler

Posted on February 4, 2007 by jim Posted in Uncategorized — No Comments ↓

The last few days have been great. Not only did we spend a couple of nice days seeing Vancouver’s sights (and not shying away from its eats and drinks either) but now we’re staying on Blackcomb mountain. The hotel‘s really great, it’s run by a seemingly unending supply of Aussies – who can’t mention the cricket at all, which I’m guessing isn’t the case in the lucky country (excluding England’s recent win of course!).

But I digress. The main event is, of course, the snow, and Whistler has had a great season so far. The day after we arrived we met up with Ian, Jan, Sue, and Rob (all accomplished skiers) and we skied/rode with them for a couple of days which was excellent (Sue has spent so much time here that it’s like being with your own full-time mountain guide). Ian was inclined to take videos of our efforts to “help improve technique” or perhaps just to send off to one of those funny home video shows. I’m guessing he’ll post something embarrassing on YouTubeâ€¦

Those guys then departed for yet another ski resort and we were left alone for a day, before Savas arrived on Friday evening with his partner. We’ve just spent the day in light snowy conditions cruising round Whistler mountain. It’s good to see him again – it’s been a year since we hung out for my wedding. Hopefully we’ll see more of each other now I’ll be living closer (really, a transatlantic flight seems like a stroll versus anything from Australia), but for now it’s time to stop with all this writing and head out for beers and food.
