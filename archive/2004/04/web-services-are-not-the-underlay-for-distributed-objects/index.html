Web Services are not the Underlay for Distributed Objects

Posted on April 22, 2004 by jim Posted in Uncategorized — No Comments ↓

Over at his blog, Jacek says that:

“Web Services are the communication infrastructure on which Distributed Objects systems can be built.”

I could not disagree more vehemently.

Certainly some services may choose to use objects as their building blocks. I suspect most services will. However exposing the implementation details of services to other services (i.e. letting objects in one service bind to objects in another service) using SOAP and WS-* protocols is wrong. It violates SOA and I am adamant that just using SOAP and friends does not mean you are “doing Web Services.” The oft-cited parallel is that you’re not building O-O software just because you’re using C++.

Services communicate via message exchanges, but if you publicly announce the binding of those message exchanges to specific objects in your system, you’ve shackled yourself to those objects forever unless you are willing to break your consumers’ applications.

One thing that I did agree on in Jacek’s post is that:

“Efforts like WS-ResourceFramework go in that direction, I think.”

Yes indeed WS-RF does try to impose an object-like view of the world, where state and identity are tightly coupled. This is not a sensible approach. As Steve points out, WS-RF is somewhat like a combination of WSDL+CORBA.
