Repeat after me: SOAP is the unit of transfer in Web Services

Posted on December 13, 2004 by jim Posted in Uncategorized — No Comments ↓

Steve Vinoski has been a
strong proponent in the WS-Addressing
WG of allowing EPRs to contain multiple addresses. Steve uses a cool
business card analogy where when he presents a business card to someone, it
contains a myriad of addressing information including phone, email, fax, and
even paper mail addresses. The absurdity of having to have a different business
card printed depending on which addresses you want to share with people seems to
underscore that point.

Since I think EPRs aren’t the sexy bit of WS-Addressing, I was really quite
neutral on this particular argument. I am a SOAP-oriented guy and I know that my
EPRs would contain addressing information for SOAP endpoints accessible over
whatever transport protocol takes my fancy – the SOAP message is the unit of
transfer in the

MEST architectural style. On the other hand Steve has good reasons for
wanting to be able to both describe arbitrary endpoint types (in WSDL) and thus
support their funky addresses in WS-Addressing. This is old news really,
Steve and I have
been around the
block on this one before – the difference is that I’m a greenfield
architect, whereas Steve has to deal with brownfield integration. With that
caveat in mind, read on.

However I’m changing my neutral status in this battle to engage on the side
of the single address per EPR people. Why? Well it seems to me that having
multiple addresses for “Jim Webber” is really annoying (of course, there are
other Jim Webbers out there that really deserve separate addresses from me). I
actually think that those people who want to communicate with me, aren’t so
fussy about how they do it. Wouldn’t it be a whole lot nicer if you could type
into your communication client “Get Jim” (not that
GETMark, stop being excited!)
and your client would initiate a phone call, dump a voicemail in MP3 format into
my email box, send me an SMS or whatever. It is because the human communication
network is so fractured and crappy that a multiplicity of addressing details for
an endpoint has to be shared.

So bringing this back into the Web Services world, we could create
WS-Addresses to deal with this situation and allow EPRs to carry all sorts of
addresses for a variety of types of endpoints. We could create, as we have done
with WSDL, a generic specification which can be used by a variety of
technologies including, with some serious effort, some of the other Web Services
technologies like SOAP.

Or conversely we could finally accept that Web Services are about SOAP not
WSDL. We could decide that the SOAP message is the unit of transfer, and not the
envelope format of the underlying transport protocol. We could route those SOAP
messages over arbitrary protocols to endpoints which accept SOAP messages and be
happy that addressing SOAP messages is the very reason that WS-Addressing
exists.

SOAP message transfer with WS-Addressing is my nirvana. Once we’re at that
rudimentary level all the other stuff just looks so surmountable.

Today’s Web Services Super-Hero: SOAP Messages with WS-Addresses

Clarification: Savas IM’d me to point out
that this posting isn’t clear and that I should have made it explicit that
multiple SOAP endpoints in an EPR (albeit potentially over different transport
protocols) is OK. What isn’t OK (at least from my MEST-ish perspective) is to
have multiple non-SOAP endpoints in an EPR (which is the generic WSDL approach
that I dislike). So I should I have said that I’m coming out in favour of a
single kind of endpoint per EPR.
