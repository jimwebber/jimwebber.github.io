GGF 11 Wrap-Up

Posted on June 11, 2004 by jim Posted in Uncategorized — No Comments ↓

For me at least the GGF 11 conference is over and I’m
now back in Sydney. The conference actually finishes today local time, but due to a prior
commitment compounded by losing a day crossing the international date line I’ve
had to leave a little early. Crossing the date line is, by the way, very
confusing.

This GGF was probably the most focussed I have been at any GGF meetings. I
pretty much kept to my own areas of interest and mostly stayed out of sessions
that were interesting from a geeky point of view, but outside my specialist
knowledge.

I
presented the
WS-GAF registry work concentrating on the underlying
architectural principles on Sunday at a workshop that
Savas and
Steve organised.
While it wasn’t explicitly mentioned, the “processThis” model formed the basis
for all of that. Yesterday I spoke at their ad-hoc BoF session on “Web Services
Grids” taking a code-monkey view of the grander visions that are being mooted.
In this session I was a bit more blatant, (remember: Web Services are deployed
at endpoints not middlepoints) and the audience was up for it too – great fun.

The Transactions RG BoF sessions went ahead too. I chaired the first of these
session and we had a solid use case presented by the
eDiamond guys by
Andrew
Martin (on behalf of
Andrew Simpson) of
Oxford University‘s
Computing Laboratory, as well as a
potential model for Grid transactions presented by Deiter Gawlick of
Oracle.
While Dieter’s work was certainly interesting from the point of view of an
(ex-?) transaction geek, I’m very keen to see the Grid transactions work
addressing real use cases. Andrew’s use case was a fairly straightforward case
of supporting the consistent update of two heterogeneous systems and looks to be
a good candidate for the deployment of existing Web Services transactioning
solutions.

I couldn’t attend the second session because it clashed with my flight home,
and I couldn’t get the session
re-scheduled. Dave Pearson of Oracle kindly stepped in for me – big cheers to
Dave for that.

The social side of the conference was a resounding success. I managed to hang
out withalltheusualsuspects, as well as physically catching up with some email
acquaintances. There was some great tech chat over food and (plenty of) beers
which was super-useful.

There’s probably a lot of digital photos out there that confirm this
conference was a belter (like

this from Daniel). I think Savas might have
captured a few moments of us at
work, but they’re definitely too few to prove anything much. Our (absent)
boss
might not approve :-)

Finally I managed to collect a few excellent funny or insightful quips from
some of the other attendees. These are edited for clarity (i.e. left over from
what I remember post-beer). I definitely loved the Millwall quip – that made me
laugh every time our paths crossed.

[Globus Team Member] : “We’re the Millwall of the Grid community –
everyone hates us and we don’t care.”
[HP staffer] : “The linux world is focussed on taking the desktop. They haven’t
realised that the desktop has moved onto the laptop.”
[Sun staffer] : “Trickle-down economics doesn’t work because in reality money
trickles up.”
