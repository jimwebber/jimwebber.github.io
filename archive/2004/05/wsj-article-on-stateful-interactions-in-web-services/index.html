WSJ Article on Stateful Interactions in Web Services

Posted on May 13, 2004 by jim Posted in Uncategorized — No Comments ↓

Savas, Mark, and I wrote an article comparing WS-Context and WS-ResourceFramework for Web Services Journal which appears in this month’s edition. While both WS-Context (which is part of the larger
WS-CAF suite of specifications) and WS-ResourceFramework deal with more than just stateful conversations, state seems to be a big issue in the Web Services world right now.

The article covers the whole spectrum of cases from simple point-to-point consumer-service interactions right the way up to distributed activities with multiple participants. The conclusions are that for simple cases WS-RF (using features from
WS-Addressing) is probably simplest, but that WS-Context simply scales a lot better in the general case.

So nothing too contentious there then :-)
