Hands off our Beeb, Mr Blair

Posted on November 9, 2004 by jim Posted in Uncategorized — No Comments ↓

Living in Australia has been a wonderful experience so far, but I have to say it isn’t all Bondi babes and barbies. In particular Australian news media is, alas, a pitiful mix of local sensationalism and “entertainment.” The quality of journalism is poor and worsening as the formely solid ABC (imagine the BBC presented by Mick Dundee) is eroded from all sides.

Fortunately for me, the BBC offers unparalleled news coverage both on its BBC World TV channel and, of course, via its excellent web site (ok, so there is also some good journalism in the SMH). However it seems the writing is on the wall for bbc.co.uk and I for one am not happy about this. Not at all.

Since the British government was vindicated over Andrew Gilligan’s shoddy display of journalism (thank you so much Andrew, sterling job – did you ever consider a career in politics where your tendancy to make things up would actually be helpful?) over the “sexing up” of an Iraqi weapons dossier, the government has become vindictive towards the BBC. And now Mr Blair and his buddies are circling for the kill.

Certainly some of the proposed changes soften the blow a little. Who can argue with making the BBC archives easier to access, or better usability for bbc.co.uk (which is already pretty good I think)? However under this set of recommendations (does that mean they can be ignored?), the seeds for the demise of the BBC’s online presence are sown:

  • At least 25% of the BBC’s online content to be supplied externally by 2006
    • Super-weak. Why? The BBC is one of (if not the) world’s greatest information gathering and reporting organisations. Why should it have to get a specific amount of its content through (presumably inferior) third parties. Whoever thought of this recommendation should be sacked pronto.
  • Link bbc.co.uk to more external, high-quality third-party content.
    • A good idea in principle. However it assumes that there is sufficient high-quality third-party content, which there isn’t. The Web is choc full of rubbish and there is very little on it which can match the quality of the BBC and still appeal to a general audience.
  • Commit to commissioning a major independent review of bbc.co.uk in three years’ time
    • Ah, the death-knell. Who will staff this independent review? The bbc.co.uk’s peers? Seems logical, oh but wait a minute its “peers” are the people who run the Guardian online and its whinging broadsheet bedfellows. Those people who would profit most from the demise of the superior publication that is bbc.co.uk.
Ok, so Eastenders online is for the chop which is a good thing. But why do our political masters insist on meddling in matters which they have no clue? Surely they should all be occupied working out how to keep the NHS running or how to extricate us from the various fronts on the *cough* war on terror rather than fixing something which patently isn’t broken.

In keeping with recent trends, does anyone have any pointers on the Canadian immigration process just in case Tony does a hatchet job on the beeb? Not sure what it’d achieve but contemplating becoming Canadian does seem to be the form of protest du jour.
