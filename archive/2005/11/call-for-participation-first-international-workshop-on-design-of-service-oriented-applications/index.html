Call for Participation: First International Workshop on Design of Service-Oriented Applications

Posted on November 20, 2005 by jim Posted in Uncategorized — No Comments ↓

The WDSOA’05 http://elab.njit.edu/wdsoa05
Workshop will be taking place later this year alongside ICSOC 2005. If you’re
going to be attending ICSOC, then I’d like to encourage you to also attend the
workshop. Read to the end of this posting for the papers that will be presented
and published.

Objectives

The transition towards service-oriented computing necessitates re-evaluation
of design methodologies that are used in the construction of enterprise applications.
Web Services design is an active research area and while there is some agreement
about the basic design principles there are no comprehensive design methodologies
for Web Services at present. There is a need for input from the research community
and industry-based practitioners in order to develop design frameworks to support
best practices for Web Services projects. The aim of this workshop is to bring
together researchers and industry experts and provide a forum for exchange of
ideas about design of Web Services and related issues.

Workshop Topics

  • Workshop topics of interest include, but are not limited to the following:
  • Design methods and guidelines for developing service-oriented applications
  • Design of industry-domain Web Services
  • Design of Web Services for enterprise application integration
  • Methods for transforming existing component-based applications to Web
    Services
  • Design methods for Web Services composition
  • Web Services design and interoperability case studies
  • Web Services design best practice frameworks
  • Architectural styles for service-oriented applications
  • Design of highly scalable service-oriented systems
  • Analysis and modelling approaches for service-oriented applications

Issues and Focus

While most observers agree about the benefits of service-oriented computing,
there is less of an agreement about suitable architectural and interaction styles
for Web Services applications. Of equal importance is the current lack of agreement
about basic design principles for making decisions about what should constitute
a service and its interface, and related considerations of service granularity.
The focus of this workshop will be on methods, frameworks and approaches for
the design of Web Services applications. The workshop will provide a forum for
the discussion of the following issues:

  • What are the key design objectives for Web Services applications?
  • Given a set of application requirements, what are the steps for transforming
    these requirements into corresponding services?
  • What constitutes a good design of service-oriented applications?
  • What is the impact of service granularity on cohesion and coupling of
    services?
  • What determines interoperability of service-oriented applications?
  • What determines scalability of service-oriented applications?
  • What are the key differences in design of intra-enterprise and inter-enterprise
    service-oriented applications?
