New Job: ThoughtWorks Australia

Posted on January 18, 2005 by jim Posted in Uncategorized — No Comments ↓

With the recent completion of the WS-GAF project, it is time for me to move on to pastures new. As Savas points out in this posting WS-GAF was a pretty successful project, and it was good fun to work on (especially since my collaborators Paul and Savas are such easy going guys). However being happy with past projects doesn’t pay the future rent, so late last year I started talking to a bunch of people about re-entering the industrial arena to practice what I’d been preaching as an academic researcher.

Ultimately I chose to throw my lot in with ThoughtWorksAustralia based on meeting some of the TW people and a strong recommendation from my buddy Anna (and of course getting through the selection process!). As Don pointed out, TW has a really good tribal feeling about it and there are some pretty smartcookies leading the technology side of things.

One of the nice things about ThoughtWorks is the fact we’re encouraged to engage with people in the community at large which for me generally means conference speaking or writing (though lots of folks here also get heavily involved with open source). This year I’m going to be talking from a TW perspective at a bunch of events. There’s the SOA conference in Sydney that I mentioned previously; the MS Regional Architect Forum in the Gold Coast where I’ll be talking about Web Services transactions (Martin is giving the keynote here); and following on from that I’ll be talking at the MS Regional Architect Forum in Beijing on patterns for dependable SOA which is tremendously exciting, especially because I’ve never been to China before.

So a busy year ahead. I expect to be gearing up on a agile and patterns and continuing with .Net and Web Services. Watch this space…
