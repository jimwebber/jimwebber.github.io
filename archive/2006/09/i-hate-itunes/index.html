I hate iTunes

Posted on September 12, 2006 by jim Posted in Uncategorized — No Comments ↓

My home workstation died a couple of weeks back. As a consequence I’ve had to move some files around onto my laptop and backup laptop, and move files from the laptops onto an external USB drive. No big deal, you’d think, even though I had to move my mp3s onto that same external disk. This shouldn’t be a big deal since I carry that disk around with me anyway to take daily backups, so being without tunes is decidedly unlikely.

Today I decided to update the playlist on my iPod nano. I have to say I love this device in its own right, but taken in combination with iTunes, it’s downright bloody draconian. You’ll recall that my mp3s now live on an external drive, so iTunes can’t find my library because its location has changed. No problem, a quick clickety-click later and  I’ve added the new folder to the library. Too easy.

Except that in some nonsense anti-consumer coding error by Apple, adding exactly the same data from a new location causes my iPod’s playlist to get reset. I am furious – it was a great playlist! To put this into perspective, Apple have invested time, effort, and money, into making sure that whenever I change the location of my media that my iPod gets reset. This is, frankly, taking the piss. Not to mention that I would quite like to sync my tunes from my laptop or my workstation depending on where I’m working whereas iTunes does the same annoying “anti-copying” trick there too. At some point Apple seems to have forgotten this is my data, on my devices!

So now I’m looking for a replacement for iTunes. I noticed (before my workstation died) that Windows Media Player 11 has some support for the iPod. I used to use ephpod in the old days before iTunes for Windows. I guess I’ll try that again since I liked the integration with the Windows shell. If anyone has any favourites I’d been keen to hear them.

Message to Apple – your player is a commodity. It’s lost its cool factor, and it’s client application is annoying as hell to use. You’ve just lost one ITMS customer because I will not be using iTunes again, and I’m sure I’m not the only person to have been stung by this. Isn’t it time you actually applied some of that “legendary usability” savvy to this? Or maybe you’ll be sticking with Apple tradition and giving you customers the one-finger (mouse) salute…
