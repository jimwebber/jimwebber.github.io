SSDL Links

Posted on August 17, 2006 by jim Posted in Uncategorized — No Comments ↓

Today I received a referral to my blog from a Google query asking “can java support ssdl“. The answer, of course, is an emphatic “yes” if someone would get round to actually building some tools (shame on us SSDL-ers for not doing so). Still the query was interesting and in re-running it, I found a bunch of stuff on the Web about SSDL that I hadn’t seen before. Here’s the lowdown:

  • Steve Loughran puts
    the boot into WSDL 2.0 and threatens to implement SSDL on Axis 2, one of these days;
  • Arjen Poutsma notes that
    SSDL is a better match for SOAP services than WSDL, but notes that it isn’t (yet) the standard in this space;
  • Alan Fekete hosts
    a copy of a presentation I gave at his EII workshop last year;
  • Daniel Diephouse says
    he wants to get rid of XML Schema and WSDL down the line, and replace it with something like
    RelaxNG and SSDL. Good choice!
  • Matthew Graham gave a
    course at the US National Virtual Observatory Summer School from last year addresses why
    doc/lit/wrapped is a bad idea as well as touching on MEST and SSDL. Good stuff;
  • On a pragmatic note, Javier CÃ¡marasays that he couldn’t care less if SSDL is better than WSDL because vendor support is all that matters. Interesting read, but I’d have to beg to differ;
  • Dan Shollernotes that SSDL focuses on the basics (the protocol) rather than the language bindings as WSDL does, and notes that SOAP is today’s sensible messaging protocol;
  • Todd Fastsays that SSDL gives operation ordering over and above WSDL. That’s not the only benefit, but it does stop a lot of trial and error;
  • Finally, Shaji Sethu
    wonders
    aloud whether WSDL should be abandoned?

Starting on an emphatic “yes” it only seems proper to end this post on an emphatic “yes” too :-)
