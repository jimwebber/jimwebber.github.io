Tech Ed New Zealand, Day 1

Posted on August 21, 2006 by jim Posted in Uncategorized — No Comments ↓

So here I am in Auckland, New Zealand for Microsoft’s sell-out (as in fully booked, rather than lacking credibility!) TechEd conference. Tomorrow I’m presenting my “Guerrilla SOA” presentation on incremental, business-driven integration, but for today I’m happily attending sessions and generally having a good time geeking out in .Net land. Last night was the speaker’s dinner, which had fabulous food some decent beers (Kiwi’s brew beer, Aussies do wine and I’ve just got to get used to that…) and sat and talked with a bunch of interesting folks like Ron Jacobs and Dave Lemphers, and caught up with Nigel Watson.

I attended a nice friendly “.Net 3.0 for duffers” presentation this morning presented by Payam Shodjai, and then a slightly more thorough introduction (with demo warts-n-all) on Windows Workflow by Paul Andrew, although Payam’s first talk was able to cover much of that ground in a very concise manner too.

Then lunch, and my earlier perceived downsides of the venue and the conference’s sell-out status became all too apparent. Earlier in the morning it had been a bit of a scrap to get from session to session (and pick up a coffee on the way). Lunchtime was much worse. The amount of people and the poor layout of the venue (actually venues – the conference is has a middle-aged spread thing going on) made it a complete crush. Now I understand why Kiwis are so good a rugby – they live the game, literally.

Still I’ve slunk off to a quiet corner for a minute to eat my “free salary survey” ice-cream (ice-cream good, which makes up for the salary shock), jot a note or two, and plan my offensive on the coffee cart for real coffee without having to deal with a crowd of scrum-half/geek hybrids to contend with. Then onto this afternoon’s sessions, and I’m planning to cram some book writing in during the breaks (otherwise Savas will get ahead of me).

So aside from he conference, it’s my first time in Auckland (indeed my first time on the North Island at all). A lot of the presenters from North America are expressing how excited they are at being in NZ for the first time – I’m not sure I have the right accent announce my excitement as vehemently (stoic, is more my thing) but Auckland seems a nice enough place. It certainly lives up to the Kiwi reputation of inventing insane ways to possibly die at high speeds – there are two death-defying/vomit inducing contraptions I can see from my hotel room. The first is a mad looking bungee catapult which fires three people in a cradle into the air and pretty much stops them from crashing straight back to the ground and dying. The other is the famous Sky Tower where those brave enough can do the Sky Jump which is like a base jump with a wire rather than a parachute. I’m not convinced I am mental enough to do either of these. Time will tell.
