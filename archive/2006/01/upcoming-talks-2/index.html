Upcoming Talks

Posted on January 18, 2006 by jim Posted in Uncategorized — No Comments ↓

I’m giving two talks in the Sydney area in February which might be of interest to Web Services geeks out there:

First up, on Wednesday 1st February I’m giving a talk called “Guerilla SOA” on how SOAP and the WS-* specs can be used to incrementally build out your integration infrastructure without resorting to buying big lumps of middleware. This talk is a repeat of the technical briefing I gave last year for ThoughtWorks and will form part of the ACS Web Services SIG calendar.

On the 9th February I’m talking to the ACS E-Commerce SIG on Web Services transactions. Incidentally this will be the first time that I’ve spoken about transactions in the Web Services space since JBoss bought Arjuna’s Web Services stack – It’s cool that the talk will change from being “this is how you might transact with 3rd parties” to “this is how your processes can transact with your business partners today.”

On a vaguely related note, it also looks like I’m heading over to the USA in mid-February to talk at ThoughtWork‘s big get-together. I’m really looking forward to that since there are an awful lot of people in TW US that I haven’t yet met and hopefully lots of service-oriented systems patterns to harvest for the book Savas and I are working on too :-)
